Four concepts that are often confused

28 Apr 2006

From Dive Into Python

statically typed language
A language in which types are fixed at compile time. Most statically typed languages enforce this by requiring you to declare all variables with their datatypes before using them. Java and C are statically typed languages.
dynamically typed language
A language in which types are discovered at execution time; the opposite of statically typed. VBScript and Python are dynamically typed, because they figure out what type a variable is when you first assign it a value.
strongly typed language
A language in which types are always enforced. Java and Python are strongly typed. If you have an integer, you can't treat it like a string without explicitly converting it.
weakly typed language
A language in which types may be ignored; the opposite of strongly typed. VBScript is weakly typed. In VBScript, you can concatenate the string '12' and the integer 3 to get the string '123', then treat that as the integer 123, all without any explicit conversion.

I like these clear and concise definitions. Hope it reduces the confusion between dynamic typing and strong typing

Why I should blog more and write better blog entries?

18 Mar 2006
"Besides a mathematical inclination, an exceptionally good mastery of one's native tongue is the most vital asset of a competent programmer."
    1. Dijsktra

Almost every competent programmer I know writes extremely well. I wish I could write as well as them. ( Maybe then I could also count myself among them ). Every programmer who is good thinks of the programs that he writes as a way of communicating his thoughts. Now I guess people who are good at communicating their thoughts in one medium are also good at doing the same in another. Well one of the reasons I started this blog was to teach myself how to write clearly and concisely. I do not think I have done a good job of this so far. Also most of my posts are just irrelevant rants. I have not really thought about what value I should be providing those who actually might read them. Most of these posts zero value.

If this blog is to serve it's purpose I must find a topic that I can write about regularly. I have a friend C++ Truths who does a pretty good job about writing about a subject near to his heart. One of my problems is that there are too many subjects near to my heart that I want to blog about. Maybe I should have two blogs a professional blog where I try to write something that is technical and interesting and a personal blog which will be password protected and available only for friends and family.
